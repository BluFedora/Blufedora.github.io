Here is a little the about us:

 

Located in Zephyrhills, Florida, Living Tayyib is a small homestead-styled farm practicing wholesome farming methods with an emphasis on utilizing local resources and employing concepts found in permaculture and other integrated farming practices.

 

  We don't grow what the public wants, or even grow the foods our own family initially wanted to eat. We grow perennial plants in our backyard that simply love to grow with great vigor, but with very little of our effort. By yielding to the local environment and maintaining an open mind as to what dinner can look like, we have been able to achieve zero use of pesticides or petroleum based fertilizers on the farm, which of course means we're spending a lot less money to grow our food.

 

  We have a small flock of poultry running free range on 4 acres. They eat the bugs and help fertilize the plants while being protected by Jada, our clever Border collie, and  Mira, our young Anatolian livestock guard dog.  We have one miniature Jersey cow with an occasional calf, and a decent school of tilapia that live in the headwater of the irrigation system used for all the crops on the farm. Our primary focus is to feed all of the animals and our family. Our secondary focus is to sell produce and livestock.  We are also hoping to increase the amount of potted plants we are making available for sale this year.

 

  We have developed some techniques that take advantage of the relationships between particular perennial plants, animals and insects. We believe these techniques can be reproduced locally by other families who want to save money while growing their own food with the least amount of effort.
